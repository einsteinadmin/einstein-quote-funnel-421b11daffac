📚 Research that informed this tool

From the low-intent lead research pass (June 2026). These findings shaped every proposed change + the routing logic.
🚪 The bar — every question or change to the quote form must clear it.
We don't add fields to look thorough. A new question or change earns a spot on the form only if it does at least one of these:
  • Helps us convert — moves more visitors to a booking.
  • Helps us de-mix — separates full / partial / storage moves inside one sqft band.
  • Helps us triage — tags lead quality so we route fast to the right path.
  • Improves the customer experience — makes the customer feel seen, not interrogated.
  • Feeds estimate accuracy — a signal the quote can learn from over time.
If a proposed change can't claim one of these, it doesn't go on the form. Friction has a cost — every field has to pay for itself.
⏱

Speed-to-lead is the #1 lever

A 5-minute vs 30-minute response is ≈ 21× more likely to qualify the lead (HBR/MIT). Einstein's current 2–6 hr reply time is the single biggest leak — and it hurts most on the hot, valuable leads. Takeaway: recovering self-serve volume + routing fast beats perfecting any score.

🎯

"Inestimable" ≠ "low intent"

A complex move is often the most valuable lead. So we don't sort by estimable/inestimable — we route on Complexity × Intent (the 2×2 above). The Complex + Hot quadrant deserves the fastest human touch, not the nurture pile.

🧭

Behavioral signals are the spine

Move-date proximity, repeat customer, repeat visitor — free, no questions added, and the most reliable. Device, time-on-form, and back-tracking are weak and B2B-derived → capture but don't weight them.

✋

Soft questions: capture, don't over-trust

"Preferred contact method" and a readiness self-select are plausible but unproven intent signals. Capture them (real routing value) and validate against actual bookings before weighting. Frame as personalization, never as a qualifying interrogation.

➕

Cap the added friction

At most ~two optional, single-tap, post-capture questions. Every field you add costs some conversion — so each one has to earn its slot.
